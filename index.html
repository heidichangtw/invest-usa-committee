About Us

Mission, activities & members

Mission statement

The Invest USA Committee is dedicated to advancing Taiwan–U.S. investment ties by helping Taiwan companies establish and expand operations in the United States. We bring together U.S. state partners, Taiwan and U.S. government-affiliated organizations, industry experts, professional service providers, and business networks to provide coordinated support and practical guidance. By connecting companies with the right resources at every stage of their investment journey, we help them successfully launch, grow, and build a long-term presence in the U.S. market.

What We Do

  • Serve as a central platform connecting Taiwan companies with U.S. investment resources
  • Bring together U.S. state economic development offices, government-affiliated organizations, and professional service providers
  • Share practical information and resources to support U.S. investment planning
  • Foster collaboration among organizations supporting Taiwan–U.S. investment

Who We Convene

  • U.S. state and regional economic development organizations
  • Government-affiliated organizations supporting cross-border investment
  • Professional service providers with U.S. market expertise
  • Industry associations and business networks
  • Taiwan companies exploring or expanding U.S. operations
  • Universities, research institutions, and innovation partners

Invest

Top 10 things to know about successfully investing in the U.S.

Source: SelectUSA Investor Guide, U.S. Department of Commerce — trade.gov/selectusa-investor-guide

For each item, click the source to learn more, or click the directory link to find a qualified professional.

01Choose the Right Business Structure

The SelectUSA Investor Guide identifies business structure as one of the first critical decisions for foreign investors, covering LLC, C-Corp, S-Corp, and branch office structures. The right choice depends on your tax situation, liability preferences, and long-term goals.

SelectUSA — Business structureMember directory: Legal Services

02Understand U.S. Tax Obligations at Every Level

The U.S. has a layered tax system spanning federal, state, and local levels. Working with a qualified CPA on corporate income tax, transfer pricing, and applicable tax treaty provisions is essential before establishing your entity.

SelectUSA — TaxationMember directory: Accounting, Audit & Tax Services

03Research Federal and State Incentive Programs

SelectUSA offers a Federal Programs & Incentives Database alongside a State Business Incentives Database. Understanding programs early — including CHIPS Act, IRA, and Opportunity Zones — can significantly reduce capital costs.

SelectUSA — Incentives Databases (Stats)SelectUSA — Fact Sheets

04Connect with State Economic Development Organizations (EDOs)

SelectUSA works with companies and EDOs to address questions involving federal regulations and activities related to investments. Engaging EDOs early is critical as incentive programs often require advance notification.

SelectUSA — EDO servicesMember directory: Government & Public Sector Organizations

05Plan Your Workforce Strategy

The U.S. workforce is skilled, innovative, and mobile — output per hour is more than 30% above the OECD average. State and local governments actively help companies understand labor regulations and workforce incentives.

SelectUSA — Workforce Data AnalysisMember directory: Human Resources & Talent Solutions

06Secure U.S. Banking and Financial Infrastructure Early

The United States hosts the most developed financial markets in the world. Opening accounts for a foreign-owned entity takes longer than expected — start early and work with banks that have Taiwan relationships.

SelectUSA — Financial marketsMember directory: Banking & Financial Services

07Develop a Capital and Funding Strategy

The SelectUSA Investor Guide covers raising capital including U.S. capital markets, venture capital, private equity, and tax considerations. U.S. capital investors are typically not involved in day-to-day management.

SelectUSA — Raising capitalMember directory: Banking & Financial Services

08Build Your U.S. Customer Base and Business Development Approach

The SelectUSA Investor Guide addresses customer acquisition best practices including B2B and B2C marketing, internet marketplaces, and public relations tactics for companies new to the U.S. market.

SelectUSA — Customer acquisitionMember directory: Business Consulting & Market Entry

09Understand Federal Procurement and Regulatory Compliance

The SelectUSA Investor Guide covers Build America, Buy American, Trade Agreements, and the Berry Amendment. Companies in semiconductors, environmental technology, or advanced manufacturing should also assess CFIUS requirements early.

SelectUSA — Federal procurementMember directory: Legal Services

10Use SelectUSA as Your Single Point of Contact

SelectUSA serves as a single point of contact at the federal level. Since inception, SelectUSA has facilitated more than US$64 billion in investment, creating and/or retaining over 86,000 U.S. jobs.

SelectUSA Investor GuideSelectUSA Investment Summit

Source: SelectUSA Investor Guide, U.S. Department of Commerce, International Trade Administration. Available at trade.gov/selectusa-investor-guide.

Invest

For U.S. state economic development organizations (EDOs) seeking Taiwanese partners and investors

Why Taiwan Is a Strategic Investment Partner for the United States

  • Taiwan and the United States have strengthened economic cooperation through the U.S.–Taiwan Agreement on Trade & Investment, supporting closer commercial engagement and long-term investment collaboration.
  • Taiwan is a global leader in advanced manufacturing, with internationally competitive strengths in semiconductors, AI supply chains, robotics, drones, HealthTech, and food manufacturing. Taiwan investment brings advanced manufacturing capabilities, technology expertise, and resilient supply chain partnerships that support innovation and high-quality job creation across the United States.

How U.S. Economic Development Organizations Can Engage

  • Join the Invest USA Committee as a participating organization.
  • Maintain an organization profile in the Committee's online directory.
  • Participate in Committee programs and networking opportunities, when available.
  • Engage with Taiwan companies through SelectUSA and other investment promotion initiatives.
